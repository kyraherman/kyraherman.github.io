Founded in early ages, Indians settled in what is now the city of Nashville. The land was used for hunting and growing crops. In the later 1700s, French fur traders occupied the area with trading posts. W hile the city boomed with trades, the first actual establishment was in 1779. James Robertson lead the group building a stockade on the Cumberland River. The name Nashville originated from Nashborough which was given in honor of Francis Nash who


won the people favor in the early stages of the town. In 1796, Tennessee was the sixteenth state added to America, making Nashville the standing capital. Nashville went through many changes throughout the next year before reaching the musical city it is today. At the time of the civil war, troops occupied much of the city. After wars died down the population increased and industry, business, and education boomed.
