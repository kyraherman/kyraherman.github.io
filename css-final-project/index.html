The Music Captial of the World

Known as the Country Music Capital of the world, Nashville is home to a little less than 700,000 people. These residents support the studious Vanderbilt University. The capital city of Tennessee fills with tourists due to all the attractions and restaurants. Nashville has many small details within the city that tells stories and creates colorful artwork. The seventh president of the United States, Andrew Jackson whose hometown was Nashville, had the driveway of his home paved in the shape of a guitar. This unique feature already tells the city had a history in music. A well known building, Grand Ole Opry, is one of the must see sights if you happen to pass through Nashville. Nashville is also home to the Western Hemisphere's tallest enclosed sculpture. This monument of Athena stands 42 feet tall inside the famous Parthenon.
